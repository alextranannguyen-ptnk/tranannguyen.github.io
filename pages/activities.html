As Head of Human Resources and Logistics and a consultant for Tran Dai Nghia’s Grade 9 Project (Jun 2022 – Aug 2023), I helped transform our final year into a collection of lasting memories. I co-edited the yearbook, produced a highlight video, and collaborated with alumni to run workshops guiding students through high school choices. Our efforts attracted over about 4,000 likes and 5,000 followers on Facebook, while also laying the groundwork for future project growth.

2SchoolGuideline is a student-led initiative designed to support 9th-grade students as they prepare for Vietnam’s highly competitive entrance exams. As Head of the Math Department, I led efforts to ensure both academic quality and emotional support. I curated over 150 math-related resources, co-developed a comprehensive exam prep handbook, and collaborated across departments to align content. I also organized two flagship workshops — Nhat Mong and Oa Thuc — where senior students shared strategies, tips, and encouragement. Through it all, my goal was simple: to make the path a little clearer for those coming next.

Eco-Empathy is an initiative by the InitiaRe Community that fosters a deeper emotional and practical connection between people and the natural world. Rooted in the idea of empathy for more-than-human life, the project encourages participants to understand environmental interdependencies, adopt nature-centered perspectives, and take actions that promote sustainability. In addition to environmental engagement, Eco-Empathy integrates technology and science education for children with chronic illnesses at the Pediatrics Hospital 2 who cannot attend school due to ongoing treatments—ensuring they remain connected to learning, creativity, and community. Through inclusive activities, education, and shared experiences, Eco-Empathy inspires responsible stewardship of the planet while empowering every member of society to thrive.

An educational program by InitiaRe Community designed for children (ages 6–14) at local shelters. Over 4 weekly sessions, 15–20 participants explored science as a space adventure, each class representing a “planet” of knowledge:Mathematics, Chemistry, Biology, Physics. The program combined hands-on experiments, creative storytelling, and interactive games, aiming to spark curiosity, build logical thinking, and nurture creativity while creating a lasting bond between volunteers and children.
